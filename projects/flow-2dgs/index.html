Flow-guided Monocular Geometry for Metric-scale Gaussian Splatting

Overview

FLOW-2DGS is a monocular 3D reconstruction framework that leverages dense optical flow to recover metric-scale scene geometry using 3D Gaussian splatting. The project aims to bridge the gap between monocular video input and high-quality, metrically accurate 3D representations.

This is the core topic of my M.S. thesis at the Robotics and Computer Vision Lab, University of Seoul.

Abstract (draft)

(석사 논문 준비가 어느 정도 진행되면 여기에 정식 초록을 넣으면 된다. 지금은 간단한 초안.)

We present FLOW-2DGS, a flow-guided 3D Gaussian splatting method that reconstructs metric-scale scene geometry from monocular videos. Dense optical flow provides pseudo-metric motion cues, which are combined with geometric priors to scale and stabilize the underlying Gaussian representation. Our approach targets robust geometry recovery in real-world dynamic and low-texture environments.

Method

The main components of FLOW-2DGS include:

  • Dense optical flow estimation between consecutive monocular frames
  • Initialization of a 3D Gaussian field from monocular cues
  • Flow-guided optimization to recover metric-scale geometry
  • Differentiable rendering of the Gaussian splats for training and evaluation
